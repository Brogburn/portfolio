About Bryce
Ogburn

Hi, My name is Bryce Ogburn. I am majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Astrophysical and Planetary Sciences at the University of Colorado Boulder. A big part of why I chose my areas of study is because of my interest in planetary studies and understanding our universe. I hope to tie the two subjects together by helping to further develop new technologies that are used for the study and modeling of data in the planetary sciences.

I like to collect star maps and other astronomical instruments from any era as I find it fascinating how well documented our understanding of the stars has been and how we've continued to grow and learn more. My love for astronomy goes as far back as I can remember with fond memories of me joining clubs in elementary school so that I could go out and use telescopes to appreciate the night sky. I didn't always know that I wanted to do computer science, however, until my first coding class at CU where I quickly discovered my passion and knew what I wanted to major in.

Now days, in my free time, I also love working with computers, often building or helping friends with building desktops. I use my extra desktops for server hosting for personal projects as well as hosting games and other sites for me and my friends. Outside of my studies, I enjoy playing games with friends (both in person and video games), enjoying the mountains of Colorado in a variety of ways such as skiing, hiking, or finding a good lake or river to go fish, and traveling with friends or family to see as much of the world as I can.
